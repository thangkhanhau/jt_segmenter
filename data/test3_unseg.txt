<p>Entîrna sentence hmasa berah hian 'kawr' neitu hi a hming sawi lang lovin, a hming âi awhtu pronoun hi posessive case atân hían kan hmang a. <p>Intransitive verb hmanna ṭawngkamah hi chuan, subject-ah hian thil titu chhinchhiahna in hman a ngai lo. Subject atâna pronoun hman chuan subject leh verb chauh hmang pawhin ṭawngkam kan chhep bawk thîn. Tunah, "A mû," tihah hian a hi subject-ah kan hmang a, chu chu verb mu-in a zui a. He mi thu pahnih hian ṭawngkam puitling siamah kan ngai thîn nain, chîk taka en chuan, hei hi, "Amah a mû," tihna a ni a. Mahse, hetah hian intransitive verb kan hman avângin thil titu amah-ah hian thil titu chhinchhiahna in dah a ngaih loh avângin, amah leh a tlukpui a hi an pahnih hniha hman theih ni mah sela, hman ve ve ṭûl kher lova kan ngaih avângin, a subject puitling zâwk tak amah lam hmaihin, pronominal a kha subject hna amahin kan thawhtîr ta a ni ber a. Mahse, transformational grammar-a deep structure-ah chuan, hetah hian amah hi telh tho a ni ang. “A mû,” tih hi chu surface structure mai a ni. Chutiang bawkin, "Keimah office-ah ka kal dâwn," tih hi deep structure ani a, "Office-ah ka kal dâwn," tih hi surface structure a ni.</p>
